AMANDA CERCAS CURRY
Amanda is a fourth-year PhD student who works with Professor Verena Rieser on detection and mitigation of abuse in social dialogue systems. Amanda believes AI development and design decisions should take a society-first approach, working towards a Hippocratic oath for AI with clearly defined ethical standards that researchers and developers should uphold. Amanda led her university’s team in the 2018 Amazon Alexa Challenge during which they were finalists out of over 200 applicants and 8 competing universities, and is a committee member of SigGen (Special Interest Group in Natural Language Generation). Amanda holds a Bachelors in Computer Science from Heriot-Watt University in Edinburgh.

Andrew Buzzell
Andrew has a long career in software engineering, most recently consulting for several high profile organizations in the sports technology space. He is also a working on a PhD in philosophy at York in Toronto, and was recently awarded a SSHRC doctoral fellowship. His research interests include issues at the intersection of cognition, tech ethics, and political epistemology.

https://twitter.com/andrewbuzzell

Michael Ralphs
Michael Ralphs is a specialist in political and social ethics. He has extensively studied the political impacts of Artificial Intelligence and responsibility structures for controlling it. He has also previously consulted with Ethical Intelligence in forecasting fintech policy discussions. This familiarity with a broad array of political issues in AI policy makes Michael a valuable member of any team evaluating the social impact of and public trust in a company’s AI policy.

Michael has presented work on responsibility and autonomous weapon systems at the Edinburgh Graduate Research Conference in Political Theory. Michael has a MSc in International Political Theory from the University of Edinburgh and a BA in Political Science and History from the University of Toronto.

https://www.linkedin.com/in/michael-ralphs-285a81183/

ALESSANDRA FASSIO
Alessandra is a keen philosopher, holding an MA (Hons) in Philosophy and an MSc in Mind, Language and Embodied Cognition from the University of Edinburgh. During her studies she developed an interest in the ethics of AI, data and technology - particularly the intersection between human (and perhaps robot?) rights, technology and the law. Her undergraduate thesis was titled 'The Moral Risks of Being too Liberal with Personhood: Discounting AI Systems from the Moral Constituency', whilst her master's project focused on 'Live Facial Recognition Technology: Privacy Invasion or a Safer Nation? An Examination of the Changing Concept of Privacy in the Digital Age.' Alessandra worked with Ethical Intelligence Ltd. as an intern during their 2019 summer programme on the Data for Children Collaborative with UNICEF project. After the internship, she was offered a job with the Collaborative and now works to help (amongst other things) review, test and finalise the ethical protocol and charter that they use for their projects. Her goal is to promote ethics as more than just a tick-box exercise, particularly in the workplace. By building on philosophical decision-making that happens organically, she hopes to encourage more thoughtful, and not forceful, ethical practice across different sectors.

https://www.linkedin.com/in/alessandrafassio1996/

Goda Mockute
Goda Mockute has background in Psychology with a focus on statistics. Her MA Psychology degree from the University of Edinburgh received an award from both the British Psychological Society and the university for academic excellence. She has various experience working with mental health (with a focus on autism spectrum disorder), science communication, academic research and teaching. Previously she has carried out research projects with Ministry of Education and Science in Lithuania and was part of EI Summer's programme. She believes that the advance of computation can provide competitive solutions to complex social challenges only if we consider the ethics and incentives behind it. Currently she is doing Behavioural Data Science pre-master's at the University of Amsterdam and volunteers for Nonprofit Ventures.

Olivia Gambelin
Olivia is an AI Ethicist who works with tech entrepreneurs to understand the importance of ethics in AI development and usage. Olivia believes that artificial intelligence offers unheard of opportunities for innovation in technology but which can only be realized through responsible and ethical practices. Olivia is originally from the Bay Area, where she started her career working in digital marketing for startups. Olivia holds an MSc in Philosophy from the University of Edinburgh, her dissertation concentration on moral responsibility and self-driving cars, a BA in Philosophy and Entrepreneurship from Baylor University, and currently sits on the Advisory Board of Tech Scotland Advocates.

https://www.linkedin.com/in/oliviagambelin/

Luke Hare
Luke is a data scientist who works with clients to provide clarity and structure to their daily business operations. Luke believes data science has a role to play in everything we do nowadays, and that the ethical implementation of technology is one of the most important challenges we face. Luke holds a MSc in Data Science from the University of Edinburgh with his dissertation concentration on Fairness and Machine Learning, and a BSc in Computational Thinking from Maynooth University.

HIROKI HIROYAMA
Hiroki is an aspiring auditor who works with business owners to better understand their companies. He believes that accountable and sustainable capitalism is possible - through reforming governance, transforming business practices and ultimately, realigning shareholder value with other stakeholder values (I.e social and environmental). He recently secured a role at BDO UK, and, has a history of managing and working with tech startups, social enterprises and charities. He is currently studying to be a chartered accountant, is a certified associate business analyst and project manager. He also holds a Masters (Hons) degree in Psychology and Philosophy from the University of Edinburgh.
