ALESSANDRA FASSIO
Alessandra is a keen philosopher, holding an MA (Hons) in Philosophy and an MSc in Mind, Language and Embodied Cognition from the University of Edinburgh. During her studies she developed an interest in the ethics of AI, data and technology - particularly the intersection between human (and perhaps robot?) rights, technology and the law. Her undergraduate thesis was titled 'The Moral Risks of Being too Liberal with Personhood: Discounting AI Systems from the Moral Constituency', whilst her master's project focused on 'Live Facial Recognition Technology: Privacy Invasion or a Safer Nation? An Examination of the Changing Concept of Privacy in the Digital Age.' Alessandra worked with Ethical Intelligence Ltd. as an intern during their 2019 summer programme on the Data for Children Collaborative with UNICEF project. After the internship, she was offered a job with the Collaborative and now works to help (amongst other things) review, test and finalise the ethical protocol and charter that they use for their projects. Her goal is to promote ethics as more than just a tick-box exercise, particularly in the workplace. By building on philosophical decision-making that happens organically, she hopes to encourage more thoughtful, and not forceful, ethical practice across different sectors.
